Ocean Facts: Frequently Asked Questions

Why are oceans salty?
Oceans are salty due to the accumulation of minerals and salts from rocks on land, carried by rivers and streams, and the evaporation process that leaves salt behind.
What is ocean acidification?
Ocean acidification refers to the decrease in pH levels of seawater, primarily caused by the absorption of excess carbon dioxide (CO2) from the atmosphere, leading to harmful effects on marine life, especially coral reefs and shell-forming organisms.
What is the Great Pacific Garbage Patch?
The Great Pacific Garbage Patch is a massive area of marine debris, primarily plastic, floating in the central North Pacific Ocean. It's one of the most notorious examples of ocean pollution.
How do oceans produce oxygen?
Oceans produce about 50-80% of the Earth's oxygen through phytoplankton, tiny marine plants that photosynthesize and release oxygen as a byproduct.
What causes ocean currents?
Ocean currents are driven by factors like wind, Earth's rotation, salinity, temperature differences, and the gravitational pull of the moon and sun.
What are dead zones in the ocean?
Dead zones are areas in the ocean with very low oxygen levels, often caused by excessive nutrient pollution from human activities, leading to a lack of marine life.
